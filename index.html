About us

Two carry-ons, no plan.

In the spring of 2022, five months before our wedding, we put everything we owned into a storage unit in Los Angeles and bought two one-way tickets to Europe. We each had a carry-on. We had no itinerary past the first two weeks.

That was the start. We thought it might be a year. We're now four years in, and counting.

Along the way the trip developed a shape. First it was just country counts — we'd never realized how vast the world was, how many places we'd never heard of. Then it was continents, then the wonders, then the world's top 100 bars, then the Michelin stars. Each new list pulled us somewhere we wouldn't otherwise have gone: the camel market in Hargeisa, the hot air balloons over the Mara, the tiny teahouse at Lobuche, the pizza counter at PST in Tokyo.

We travel carry-on only. We favor Aman and Belmond and Four Seasons for the places they can take you. We try to stay long enough in each country to leave the capital, eat at least one memorable meal, and meet people who actually live there. And we write it all down — every country page on this site is our own notes, our own photos, our own honest take.

The remaining twenty-eight are the hardest ones. North Korea. Libya. Equatorial Guinea. Chad. Central African Republic. We'll get there.
